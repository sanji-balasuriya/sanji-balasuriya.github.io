MAT205/ES205: Environmental Modeling (Connecticut)
Comment: These evaluations reflect the related issues - which had been discussed within both Mathematics and Environmental Studies - as to whether Calculus I would need to be a prerequisite or not, and whether MAT205 would qualify for satisfying the general education requirement in mathematics. In view of this feedback, I believe that Calculus I would need to be specified as a firm prerequisite for this course to be most effective.

MATHS2202: Engineering Mathematics 1 (Adelaide)

MATHS1012: Mathematics 1B (Algebra) (Adelaide)
Comment: This was a class with 600 students, which was taught in two different streams of about 300 students each, in an enormous lecture theater with no board space whatsoever. Being a course designed by a committee which had formulated lecture notes, the instructor was not permitted to change a word of the material, and was expected to be the mouthpiece who went through these notes verbatim. This lack of control of the material and the mechanism for presentation (i.e., as a lecture - which was video-recorded - with student participation essentially discouraged) made this, from my perspective, an ineffective method of teaching as is clear from the "Course" evaluations. The issues I had some control over are related to the "evaluations of Sanjeeva Balasuriya" in pages 5-11 and 14-15 (though I didn't even have control of quite a few of the aspects listed here); my efforts within these constraints led to, apparently, unprecedentedly high numbers for the instructor performance.
